The Paradox: Displacement vs. Job Creation
  • The "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" narrative on Substack has shaped consensus expectations of job destruction — yet Citadel data shows software engineer job postings +11% YoY.
  • Historical analog: every major technology displacement cycle (industrial revolution, PC, internet) created more jobs than it destroyed over a 5–10 year horizon.
  • Near-term pain is real for specific worker cohorts. The speed of AI capability improvements means this cycle may be faster and more concentrated than prior transitions.
  • For equity markets, the key transmission mechanism is: How quickly does AI capex translate into productivity, margin expansion, and EPS growth? The bull thesis requires this to happen by 2027.
Implications for Investment Positioning
  • AI capex at 2% of GDP is not cyclical — it is structural. This is the most important data point for the bull case. The investment base is too large to unwind quickly.
  • Near-term inflationary pressure from AI capex is a real macro risk. $650B in AI spending competes for scarce skilled labor and energy — upward pressure on wages and electricity prices.
  • The "displacement narrative" vs. actual data divergence suggests markets may be pricing in too much AI negative impact on enterprise software (e.g., WDAY's multiple compression may be excessive).
  • Physical AI / Robotics: GS highlighted this as a key NVDA GTC topic. If physical AI displaces workers in manufacturing, the capex cycle extends beyond data centers into new end markets.
Citadel's Bottom Line (Feb 24, 2026)
We see the near-term dynamics around the AI capex story as inflationary, but given markets are focused on the forward narrative, the constructive end-state take is warranted. The question isn't whether AI creates value — it's whether that value accrues to equity holders faster than the market has priced in at current multiples.
