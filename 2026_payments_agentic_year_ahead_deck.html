Core thesis

Moats shift from “checkout UX” to trust, tokens & risk rails.

Agentic purchasing makes checkout invisible. The economic battleground becomes authentication, fraud/chargeback controls, and network-level standards—while many incumbents face pricing downshifts.

2026 is a product + security arms race: winners monetize risk; laggards see share and valuation pressure.

Why the risk is structural

UW: PYPL

If agents optimize purchases, the consumer no longer “chooses PayPal.” That compresses the branded checkout advantage.

  • Branded share loss + price pressure: report argues PYPL has been too slow improving branded checkout, increasing competitive pressure.
  • Agentic overhang: even gradual adoption caps the multiple due to terminal-moat uncertainty.
  • Venmo optionality fades: slow acceptance build + competition reduces monetization runway.
  • Catalyst risk: potential medium-term framework reset (report references ~3.3% TM dollar growth in ’27).

Non-obvious takeaway: the biggest earnings risk can come from defensive behavior (pricing + incentives + opex) before share visibly collapses.

EW group

The report’s contention: investors overestimate the durability of price-up + software attach playbooks in SMB.

Non-obvious takeaway: market may underprice a competitive reset even with low multiples because the growth algorithm itself changes.

Stablecoins: adoption begins, displacement is constrained
Economics reality check

Where stablecoins win first

2026 setup

The report expects stablecoins to move beyond trading—but earliest volume is where instant clearing and uncertainty matter.

  • Best-fit use cases: unknown timing/amount + immediate settlement (betting; off-hour tokenized trading).
  • Cross-border impact limited (near term): focus is SWIFT alternatives; consumer usage initially low.
  • Market is already concentrated: ~$285B cap; +50% YoY from ~$190B; Tether ~65%, Circle ~27%.
  • Regulatory catalyst: GENIUS/CLARITY Acts can open regulated channels and accelerate institutional adoption.

Non-obvious takeaway: stablecoins must beat all-in costs (including off-ramp), not just FX spreads.

what to track

downside screens
  • PYPL: evidence of pricing/incentives rising to defend share; any medium-term framework revision.

regulatory
  • GENIUS/CLARITY progress: ability for regulated intermediaries to hold/use stablecoins at scale.
