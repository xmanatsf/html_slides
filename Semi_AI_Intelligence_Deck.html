Rebuttal #1: AI ROI is Real & Underestimated

Meta revenue per employee +90% over 3 years. Google, Amazon, Microsoft: +35–45%. Block announced AI enabling 40% headcount reduction. The productivity gains are empirical, not theoretical. Returns are being delivered in the current infrastructure wave, not promised in the future.

Rebuttal #2: OpEx Savings Fund CapEx

Only 1.5% savings on $60 trillion of global corporate OpEx justifies the entire $850bn AI capex cycle. In other words, hyperscalers don't need FCF to fund AI — they're redirecting existing operating budgets. FCF decline in 2026 is not a funding constraint, it's a reinvestment decision.

Rebuttal #3: Agentic AI = New Demand Paradigm

Old compute: demand limited by number of people (PCs, smartphones). New compute: AI agents interact with other AI agents — in AI-agent-only communities (e.g., Moltbot). As humans deploy more agents, compute demand grows parabolically. The human count no longer limits AI compute consumption.

UBS: AI Accelerator Units Growing Through 2027

AI accelerator units: +30% YoY in 2026E, +29% YoY in 2027E. HBM bits: +79% in 2026E, +57% in 2027E. UBS's initial 2027 supply chain work points to steady growth — no cliff. Agentic AI and AI coding deployment are the key applications to watch for demand pull-through.

Sovereign AI: Nascent but Accelerating

Sovereign AI demand reached ~15% of NVDA FY26 run-rate. Governments are building national AI infrastructure separate from hyperscaler capex. This represents an incremental demand layer not captured in standard hyperscaler models — and is still in early innings globally.

Bear Case to Monitor: FCF Sustainability

Hyperscaler FCF projected to decline/go negative in 2026 if capex continues at this pace. If AI monetization disappoints (i.e., OpEx savings don't materialize at scale), the investment thesis could face a sharp capex reversal. This is the primary structural risk to the cycle.

Sources: Evercore ISI (M. Lipacis, Feb 27, 2026) · UBS APAC Tech Strategy (N. Gaudois, Feb 11, 2026)
